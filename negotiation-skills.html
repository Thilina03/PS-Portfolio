Lecture 12 - NEGOTIATION SKILLS

Effective negotiation skills are essential in various aspects of life, including business, personal relationships, and conflict resolution.

OBJECTIVES OF NEGOTIATION

  • Preserving Relationships.
  • Resolving Conflicts.
  • Achieving better Agreements.
  • Building Trust and Rapport.
  • Gaining Competitive Advantage.
  • Adapting to different situations.
  • Promoting Social change.

ESTABLISH CLEAR OBJECTIVES

  • Prioritize your Goals.
  • Quantifying when possible.
  • Know your Walk-away point.

NEGOTIATE EFFECTIVELY

BATNA – Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement is the course of action that a person can take if the current negotiation ends without an agreement. In other words, a Plan B.

BATNA PROCESS

  • Step 1 : Identify your current situation.
  • Step 2 : Evaluate your BATNA.
  • Step 3 : Assess your current negotiation.
  • Step 4 : Determine your reservation point.
  • Step 5 : Use BATNA in the negotiation.
  • Step 6 : Negotiate based on BATNA.

ZOPA – Zone Of Possible Agreement

It is the range in a negotiation within which a deal that satisfies both parties can be reached. It is crucial for a successful negotiation because it indicates the potential for an agreement that both parties find acceptable.

ZOPA PROCESS

  • Step 1 : Identify your Aspiration range.
  • Step 2 : Identify other party’s Aspiration range.
  • Step 3 : Determine the overlapping range.
  • Step 4 : Prepare and communicate effectively.
  • Step 5 : Engage in negotiation.
  • Step 6 : Reach an agreement.
