Professional skills: Which ones are most coveted by companies?

16 July 2024
Esade Careers

In today’s increasingly competitive job market, professional skills have emerged as pivotal for career success. Employers seek candidates equipped not only with the technical competencies essential for the role but also with the requisite skills to execute their duties proficiently.

This article by the Esade Careers team analyses the professional skills most valued by companies today.

The most coveted professional skills

A study conducted by the World Economic Forum revealed that the professional skills highly esteemed by companies in 2023 encompass the following:

Complex problem solving

The capacity to address intricate issues stands as a cornerstone skill across all professions. Employers seek individuals capable of identifying and analyzing problems, devising innovative and efficient solutions, and implementing them effectively within their respective domains.

Creative thinking

Creative thinking stands as another pivotal skill today. Companies actively pursue individuals capable of thinking outside conventional boundaries and fostering innovative concepts. This skill is indispensable for driving innovation, resolving challenges, and spearheading the creation of novel products and services.

Technological literacy

Tech literacy describes the ability to use information and communication technologies adeptly. Employers seek candidates who possess knowledge of cutting-edge technologies and can leverage them to enhance productivity and efficiency within the company.

Self-efficacy skills

Self-efficacy skills encompass abilities that empower employees to oversee their own learning, development, and job performance. Companies require individuals capable of taking initiative, learning from setbacks, and embracing change.

Social-emotional attitudes

Social-emotional attitudes denote the qualities that enable employees to engage proficiently with others. Companies are in pursuit of individuals who can communicate with clarity and efficacy, collaborate seamlessly within teams, resolve conflicts amicably, and inspire and motivate their peers.

Other well-valued professional skills

Besides the basic skills mentioned above, companies are also increasingly valuing the following skills:

  • Systems thinking: This refers to the capability to comprehend the interconnections among various components within a system. This proficiency is indispensable for navigating the intricate and dynamic challenges prevalent in today’s world.
  • Artificial intelligence and big data: This involves the ability to leverage artificial intelligence and big data analytics to examine data and make informed decisions. These proficiencies are progressively crucial in an era inundated with data across all facets of our lives.
  • Talent management: The proficiency to identify, cultivate, and retain talent. This skill is indispensable for companies striving to excel in a competitive market.
  • Service and customer orientation: This entails the capability to deliver high-quality service to customers. This skill is imperative for any company aspiring to meet the demands of its customer base effectively.

Other relevant professional skills

In addition to the skills outlined above, there are other competencies pertinent to today’s labor market. These include:

  • Adaptability: Companies seek candidates capable of adjusting to change and novel situations seamlessly.
  • Resilience: Companies seek candidates who can rebound from setbacks and failures, demonstrating the ability to persevere and progress forward.
  • Organization and planning: Companies seek candidates who can effectively structure and strategize their work to optimize efficiency.
  • Time management: Companies prioritize candidates who demonstrate efficient time management skills.
  • Foreign languages: Foreign language proficiency is becoming increasingly vital in the globalized job market.
  • Digital skills: Digital skills are essential for any professional striving to keep up with today’s work environment.

Skills in decline

While no skill is undergoing a net decline, significant portions of companies perceive certain skills to be decreasing in importance for their workforce:

  • Reading, writing, and mathematics: While still crucial, they are gradually decreasing in relative importance as technology automates tasks that rely on these foundational skills.
  • Global citizenship: This encompasses the ability to comprehend and adapt to diverse cultures and environments. This competition continues to be important for companies operating in the global market.
  • Sensory processing skills: These involve the capability to perceive and interpret information from the physical world. These skills are crucial for tasks demanding a heightened level of coordination and physical dexterity.
  • Manual dexterity, endurance, and precision: These skills are paramount for tasks demanding a high level of physical adeptness.
