Lecture 07 - FOOD FESTIVAL

The food festival was conducted by the 2nd year 2nd semester batch as an assignment of our Professional Skills module. This was a group task and there were many groups with their stalls selling a variety of food and using various strategies to sell them.

OUR STALL AND TEAM

Our group, Group Alpha, had 10 members, and we sold Corn Dog, Sandwich, Masala Chai, Popcorn, Pizza, Hotdog, and Mini Cake. Each member was assigned specific tasks to ensure the success of our stall. Within just one hour, we sold all the items we had prepared, achieving the goals we had set during the planning stage.

THE PROCESS

Tasks were divided efficiently among the team. Two members were responsible for purchasing ingredients and packaging materials. Three members focused on preparing the food. On the day of the festival, some members handled packing and serving the items to customers. Others were assigned to design posters and flyers for marketing and promoting our stall.

OPERATIONS ON THE DAY

The remaining members stayed at the stall from start to finish, selling the items, handling money, giving change, and interacting with customers. Team members actively invited passersby to try our products, which resulted in a steady flow of students and staff visiting our stall throughout the festival.

LOCATION, STRATEGY AND RESULTS

We were fortunate to have our stall positioned in front of the elevators, attracting the attention of everyone coming out from them. Some team members focused on decorating the stall to make it more appealing, while others prepared and served the food. The variety of items we offered was unique and perfectly suited for lunchtime, which encouraged more people to stop by. We also used strategies such as slightly reducing the price of the last few items while maintaining our profit margin to sell all units.
