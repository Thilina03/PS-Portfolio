Lecture 02 - CV Writing

A CV is a comprehensive document that outlines your education, skills, professional experience, and key achievements. Its main purpose is to showcase your strengths and demonstrate why you are a strong fit for a specific role. A well-prepared CV helps you create a positive impression and significantly improves your chances of being shortlisted for interviews.

CV STRUCTURE AND FORMATTING

  • Use a clear, organized layout
  • Apply headings, bullet points, and white space effectively
  • Choose appropriate font and size
  • Maintain consistent formatting throughout

ESSENTIAL CV COMPONENTS

Personal Information

Contact details and professional title

Professional Summary

Compelling overview of your career and key strengths

Work Experience

Chronological list of relevant work history with achievements

Education

Academic qualifications and certifications

Skills

Technical and soft skills relevant to the position

Achievements and Awards

Notable accomplishments and recognitions

AVOIDING COMMON MISTAKES

  • Spelling and Grammatical errors
  • Including irrelevant or outdated information
  • Being overly verbose or too brief
  • Lack of consistency in formatting
