Lecture 09 - EMAIL WRITING

Email is a fast and efficient way of exchanging messages through the internet. It allows people to share written content, attachments such as documents or images, and even hyperlinks. Today, email is one of the most widely used forms of communication in education, business, and daily life because it is simple, quick, and reliable.

SUBJECT LINE

Be clear and specific about the topic of the email. The subject line should be the main point of the email. Do not include a greeting, such as “hello” or “greetings.”

GREETINGS

For all formal e-mail communication use: Dear + Recipient’s Title + Last name.

  • Dear Professor Gordon
  • Dear Mr. Gordon
  • Dear Ms. Green

TONE

  • Polite and respectful – Use courteous language and show respect.
  • Professional – Avoid slang or casual language; keep it formal.
  • Clear and concise – Be direct but respectful, without unnecessary detail.
  • Non-demanding – Make requests gently, not as commands.
  • Grateful – Always thank the recipient for their time or help.

BEFORE YOU HIT ‘SEND’

  • Check name and greeting.
  • Use proper tone and language.
  • Review spelling and grammar.
  • Include a subject line and closing.
  • Read carefully and answer fully.
  • Be calm.
  • Reply appropriately.

AFTER YOU HIT ‘SEND’

  • Remember, emails are not for issues that are time sensitive.
  • If you have sent an email after 5pm or on the weekend, you shouldn’t expect a response until the recipient returns to work.
  • If you have asked for assistance, a follow-up email may be necessary.
