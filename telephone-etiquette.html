Lecture 11 - TELEPHONE ETIQUETTE

The telephone is one of the most important and commonly used tools in business. Multitudes of businesses, companies, and departments use telephones in their work every day; however, most of us don’t think of the telephone as a tool, and as a result, accidentally misuse it.

When answering the telephone…

  • Always try to answer your own telephone whenever possible.
  • Always practice answering your telephone within 2–3 rings.

To greet the caller…

  • LARGER ORGANIZATIONS – “Thank you for calling (dept. name). How may I direct your call?”
  • SMALLER ORGANIZATIONS – “Thank you for calling (dept. name). May I help you?”
  • DEPARTMENTS – “(dept. name), Mary Smith,” OR “Mary Smith, may I help you?”

When placing a caller on HOLD…

  • Always ask, “May I put you on hold?” and wait for a response.
  • If you must place them on hold abruptly, say, “Please hold” first.
  • Check back every 30–45 seconds and offer options if it will take longer.

When you are transferring a phone call…

  • Explain to the caller why you’re transferring the call.
  • Ask for their permission before transferring.
  • Confirm the other party can take the call.
  • Return to the caller with the name, department, and number (if possible).

When taking messages…

  • Name of person for whom the message was left.
  • Caller’s name (get the correct spelling), company or dept. and number.
  • Date and time.
  • Message.
  • Action to be taken (i.e., “Please Call,” “Will call back,” or “URGENT”).

When returning a phone call…

Playing phone tag can be frustrating, so to avoid returning phone calls to a person who is unavailable, try establishing specific times to call back or try asking, “When is the best time for me to call again?” or “When is the best time for them to call me back?”

CHAPTER 10 - DINING ETIQUETTE

WHAT IS DINING ETIQUETTE?

Dining etiquette refers to the set of rules that govern polite behavior while eating.

  • Involves table manners, utensil use, and interaction with others
  • Ensures a respectful and enjoyable dining experience.

NAPKIN ETIQUETTE

  • Place the napkin on your lap after the host does.
  • Fold it neatly and use clean surfaces.
  • Do not use as a bib or crumple it.

CUTLERY PLACEMENT SIGNALS

  • Finished: Fork and knife together at 4 o’clock.
  • Pause: Knife and fork crossed.
  • Don’t place used cutlery back on the table.
